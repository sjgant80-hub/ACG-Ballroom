Middlemist et al. 1976 — the buffer is measurable

The buffer urinal protocol is not mere etiquette. Middlemist, Knowles, and Matter (1976) demonstrated that interpersonal proximity at urinals measurably delays micturition onset and reduces flow duration. Personal space invasion produces sympathetic arousal that inhibits the parasympathetic relaxation required for micturition. The buffer protocol is a physiological response to an architectural failure, not a cultural preference. Published in the Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 33(5), 541–546. One of the most cited field experiments in social psychology.
